March 2026

**1. Executive Summary**

The acupuncture-vet.co.uk website currently has no blog section. This is
a missed opportunity. The site performs well on branded searches and
benefits from strong Google reviews, but it captures virtually none of
the high-intent informational traffic from pet owners researching
acupuncture for their animals.

This strategy outlines a focused blog content plan designed to capture
organic search traffic from owners actively researching veterinary
acupuncture. The content stays tightly focused on acupuncture and
related conditions rather than broader Concierge Vet services, with the
natural funnel doing conversion work once visitors are on-site.

The plan covers 12 priority articles across three phases, writing
guidelines, author strategy, and SEO targeting. Each article targets
specific long-tail keywords that pet owners are actively searching for,
with low competition from the sparse, often clinical content currently
ranking.

**2. Current Site Audit & SEO Opportunity**

**2.1 Current State**

The acupuncture-vet.co.uk site is a WordPress site using the Stockholm
theme. Current pages are: Home, Our Service, Acupuncture, For Vets,
Contact, and FAQs. No blog exists. The site has strong testimonials (25+
Google reviews) and good branded authority, but zero informational
content for search engines to index.

**2.2 Competitive Landscape**

Looking at what currently ranks for veterinary acupuncture searches in
the UK, most competing content falls into a few categories: generic
practice service pages with thin content (Hilltop Vets, Avon Vets),
referral hospital pages (Fitzpatrick Referrals), professional body
information (ABVA), and US-centric articles (AKC, VCA Hospitals, IVAS).
Very few UK-based practices have dedicated, in-depth educational blog
content about veterinary acupuncture. This is the gap.

**2.3 The Opportunity**

Your unique advantages for SEO content:

-   Two qualified ABVA-registered veterinary acupuncturists with 47+
    combined years of veterinary experience

-   Mobile/home-visit model provides unique clinical perspective that
    clinic-based vets cannot offer

-   Treat dogs, cats AND horses, opening three distinct search verticals

-   Real clinical stories and outcomes from hundreds of patients

-   78 vet surgeries referring to you validates clinical credibility

-   Almost zero quality competition in UK veterinary acupuncture blog
    content

**5. Content Plan: 12 Priority Articles**

Articles are organised into three phases. Phase 1 captures the
highest-volume, most commercially relevant searches. Phase 2 builds
authority with condition-specific deep dives. Phase 3 expands into
underserved niches.

**Phase 1: Foundation (Articles 1-4)**

Publish within first 4 weeks. These target the highest-volume searches
and answer the questions every potential client has before booking.

**Article 1: Does Acupuncture Really Work for Dogs?**

**Author:** Dr Alastair Greenway BVM&S MRCVS ABVA

**Target keywords:** acupuncture for dogs, does dog acupuncture work,
veterinary acupuncture evidence, dog acupuncture UK

**Search intent:** Sceptical owner researching whether acupuncture is
legitimate before investing

**Word count:** 1,500-1,800 words

**Voice:** Science-grounded, pragmatic. Acknowledge scepticism directly
and address it with evidence.

Content should cover: the Western scientific explanation (endorphins,
pain-gate theory, neurophysiology), clinical evidence from research,
conditions it works best for, what to realistically expect, and why
results vary. Draw on 25 years of clinical observation. Avoid
overselling. Honest about limitations builds more trust than promises.

**Article 2: What to Expect at Your Pet\'s First Acupuncture Session**

**Author:** Dr Claire Greenway BVM&S MRCVS ABVA

**Target keywords:** first pet acupuncture session, what happens dog
acupuncture, pet acupuncture what to expect, acupuncture session dogs

**Search intent:** Owner who has booked or is about to book, wants to
know what will happen

**Word count:** 1,200-1,500 words

**Voice:** Reassuring, warm, practical. Written from Claire\'s
perspective of treating nervous animals and anxious owners.

Content should cover: the consultation and assessment process, how
needles are placed, typical reactions (many animals relax or fall
asleep), session duration, how many sessions to expect, the advantage of
home visits for reducing stress, and what owners should do during
treatment. Include real observations from practice.

**Article 3: Acupuncture for Arthritis in Dogs: A Vet\'s Perspective**

**Author:** Dr Alastair Greenway BVM&S MRCVS ABVA

**Target keywords:** acupuncture arthritis dogs, dog arthritis
acupuncture, acupuncture for old dog arthritis, arthritis treatment dogs
alternative

**Search intent:** Owner of arthritic dog looking for treatment options
beyond or alongside NSAIDs

**Word count:** 1,800-2,200 words

**Voice:** Clinical authority with compassion. This is the cornerstone
condition article.

Content should cover: why arthritis is the most common condition treated
with acupuncture, how it works specifically for joint pain, the role
alongside conventional medication, cases where it reduces NSAID
dependency, what improvement looks like in practice, maintenance
schedules, and breed considerations. This article should rank for the
highest-value commercial keywords.

**Article 4: How Much Does Veterinary Acupuncture Cost?**

**Author:** Dr Claire Greenway BVM&S MRCVS ABVA

**Target keywords:** veterinary acupuncture cost UK, how much dog
acupuncture, pet acupuncture price, acupuncture for dogs price UK

**Search intent:** Owner comparing costs, checking if insurance covers
it

**Word count:** 1,000-1,300 words

**Voice:** Transparent, helpful, non-salesy. Owners appreciate cost
transparency.

Content should cover: typical cost ranges (initial consultation vs
follow-up), factors affecting price (travel, species, complexity),
insurance coverage (most policies cover it when performed by a vet),
value comparison vs long-term medication costs, what\'s included in a
session fee, and how the initial course vs maintenance schedule affects
total cost. Be upfront about your own pricing.

**7. SEO & Technical Recommendations**

**7.1 Blog Page Structure**

The blog should live at acupuncture-vet.co.uk/blog with individual
articles at acupuncture-vet.co.uk/blog/\[slug\]. Add a \'Blog\' link to
the main navigation between \'Acupuncture\' and \'For Vets\'. Each
article page should include: breadcrumbs, author bio with photo, related
articles, a clear CTA to the contact page (not a hard sell, just a
natural \'If you think acupuncture might help your pet, get in touch\'),
and social sharing buttons.

**7.2 On-Page SEO Checklist**

-   Title tag: Primary keyword near the front, under 60 characters

-   Meta description: Compelling, 150-160 characters, includes primary
    keyword

-   H1: One per page, matches title tag intent

-   H2/H3 subheadings: Use secondary keywords naturally

-   Internal links: Each article links to 2-3 other blog posts and to
    the service/acupuncture page

-   Images: Original photos where possible (animals during treatment,
    home visits). Alt text with keywords.

-   Schema markup: Article schema with author, date published, date
    modified

-   URL slugs: Short, keyword-rich (e.g.
    /blog/acupuncture-arthritis-dogs)

**7.3 Internal Linking Strategy**

Each blog article should link to: the main Acupuncture service page, the
Contact page (naturally within content), and 2-3 other blog articles.
The existing Acupuncture page and FAQs page should also link back to
relevant blog articles once published. This creates a topical cluster
around veterinary acupuncture that signals authority to search engines.

**7.4 Content Refresh Cycle**

After the initial 12 articles, review and update every 6 months. Add new
research findings, update pricing, refresh seasonal references. Google
rewards updated content. Plan a second batch of 6-8 articles for months
5-8 based on what the first batch shows in Search Console data (which
keywords are gaining traction, which queries you\'re appearing for but
not yet ranking highly).

**9. Measuring Success**

Track these metrics monthly once the blog is live:

-   Organic search traffic to blog pages (Google Search Console +
    Analytics)

-   Keyword rankings for target terms (check monthly)

-   Click-through from blog to Contact page (set up as conversion goal)

-   Blog-to-enquiry attribution (track \'How did you find us?\'
    responses mentioning articles)

-   Average time on page and scroll depth (engagement quality)

-   Impressions and CTR in Search Console for target queries

Expect to see initial ranking signals within 4-8 weeks of publishing.
Meaningful organic traffic should build from month 3-4 onwards. The low
competition in this space means well-written, authoritative content
should rank relatively quickly.
